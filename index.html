About Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also known as the Lion's Rock, is an ancient rock fortress located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. Built in the 5th century by King Kashyapa, Sigiriya is famous for its stunning frescoes, landscaped gardens, and the unique architecture that crowns the top of the rock. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it is one of the most iconic and fascinating sites in Sri Lanka.

Key Attractions

Sigiriya Frescoes

The famous frescoes of Sigiriya are located in a sheltered pocket of the rock. These vibrant paintings depict beautiful maidens, symbolizing both spirituality and the natural beauty of Sri Lanka.

The Water Gardens

One of the most sophisticated landscaped gardens of the ancient world, the Water Gardens of Sigiriya include pools, fountains, and elaborate pathways.

Lion's Staircase

The grand entrance to Sigiriya is a giant carved lion. Only the paws remain today, but this unique feature gives Sigiriya its name, the Lion's Rock.
